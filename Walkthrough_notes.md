# Walkthrough Notes

## Project Approach

The project focuses on solving a common marketing reporting problem:
teams often need to manually collect data from multiple platforms before understanding campaign performance.

Instead of building a large analytics platform, the solution focuses on creating a lightweight and centralized dashboard that simplifies performance monitoring.

---

## Why These Visuals Were Chosen

The dashboard uses:
- KPI cards for quick performance overview
- Bar charts for campaign and channel comparison
- Line charts for monthly trend analysis
- Audience charts for customer insights

These visuals were selected because they allow users to quickly identify important performance patterns without overwhelming the dashboard.

---

## Why the Dashboard Was Kept Simple

The first version intentionally avoids excessive features and complex analytics.

The goal of the dashboard is to:
- Improve reporting consistency
- Reduce manual analysis time
- Support faster decision-making

A simpler dashboard improves usability and adoption for internal teams.

---

## Why Overengineering Was Avoided

Features such as:
- AI recommendations
- Predictive forecasting
- Real-time streaming
- Automated optimization

were intentionally excluded from the first version.

These features require more advanced infrastructure, validation, and historical data.

The focus of v1 is delivering immediate value through clear insights and centralized reporting.

---

## Key Decisions Made

- Prioritized cross-channel visibility
- Focused on decision-support insights
- Used Power BI for fast visualization and reporting
- Chose a simple dashboard structure for usability
- Focused on business insights instead of advanced engineering complexity
